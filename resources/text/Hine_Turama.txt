The celestial narratives of Hine Turama, mother of the stars, and Hine-te-āhuru, mother of the sun and moon are portrayed here.  Located above the entryway into the campus, these installations represent a modern expression of the traditional pare, being the carved mantel located above the doorway of a wharenui, where we celebrate mana wahine, the feminine dimension and influence, as the essential gateway into the whare.   We look to these figures for inspiration and to enhance our understanding of all the wonders of the heavens.
